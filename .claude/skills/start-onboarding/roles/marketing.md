# Role card - Head of marketing / content / brand / growth

Used by Phases 2, 4, and 5 when the seat runs the company's audience-facing work: content, campaigns, brand, growth, community.

## Seat interview (Phase 2)

1. **"What does marketing exist to do at [company]? Not the textbook answer - what does the owner actually expect from your department?"** → department mission.
2. **"What 2 to 4 numbers is your seat measured on - leads, pipeline you source, views, subscribers, whatever counts here?"** → KPIs.
3. **"Who do you answer to on marketing calls - the owner directly, or someone in between?"** → answers to.
4. **"Who lands requests on your desk, and what do they ask for? Sales wanting collateral, the owner wanting posts, product wanting launches?"** → incoming-request map (also feeds Phase 5).
5. **"What can you publish or spend without sign-off, and where's the line? Posts, paid budget, anything with the company name on it."** → approval boundary.

## Recommended connectors (Phase 4)

After the universal spine (google-workspace; backup is built in, nothing to set up):

- `apify-youtube` - "Paste any YouTube link and I'll have read the whole video before you finish your coffee - competitor breakdowns and research on tap." Near-mandatory for this seat.
- `telegram-notify` - "Your phone buzzes when a research job or draft is ready, even when you're away from the desk."
- `dropbox-rclone` - offer if they work with footage, audio, or a big asset library: "I can see and organise your media library without downloading any of it."
- `higgsfield` - offer only if the brand publishes face-forward content of a specific person: "Professional AI images of [person] for posts and thumbnails, without a photo shoot."
- Install-team tier to name if it comes up: `company-brain` + `worker` for live content dashboards and scheduled pipelines.

## First tasks + pack (Phase 5)

**Pack recommendation:** `content-marketing` - "The full content department in one install: video research and packaging, podcast production, social content, repurposing, trend intelligence - pre-built and tested." This is the FULL pack (21 skills); reassure them the skills for channels they don't use stay quietly dormant - the pack's own setup asks which channels are actually in play.

**First-task prompt sheet** (if they're stuck picking):
- The competitor content breakdown: what's working in the niche this month.
- Repurposing one strong piece into every channel's format.
- The content calendar: what publishes where, this week and next.
- Draft-in-their-voice for the channel they post on most.
- The monthly numbers pull: what performed, what flopped, what to double down on.
