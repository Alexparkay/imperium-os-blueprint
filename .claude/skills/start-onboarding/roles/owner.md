# Role card - Owner / founder (also solo operators)

Used by Phases 2, 4, and 5 when `user_role_type` is `owner` or `solo`. The owner's seat covers the whole company, so the seat interview is about where THEY spend attention, not about one department.

**Solo operators:** everything here applies; read "department" as "the whole business" and skip anything about delegating. A one-person company still has an approval boundary - it's the list of things they want a pause-and-confirm on before anything sends.

## Seat interview (Phase 2)

1. **"Your seat covers everything, so let's narrow it: what do YOU personally hold that nobody else can - the calls only you make?"** → seat mission.
2. **"What 2 to 4 numbers tell you the business is healthy? The ones you'd check from a beach."** → KPIs.
3. **"Who do you answer to, if anyone - a board, investors, a co-owner, or nobody?"** → answers to.
4. **"What lands on your desk that shouldn't? The asks that eat your week."** → incoming-request map (also feeds Phase 5).
5. **"What should NEVER go out without your personal sign-off - and what have you fully delegated?"** → approval boundary. For solo: "What should I always pause and confirm with you before it leaves the building - sending, posting, paying?"

## Recommended connectors (Phase 4)

After the universal spine (google-workspace; backup is built in, nothing to set up):

- `telegram-notify` - "Your phone buzzes with the result when I finish a job, even when you're away from the desk."
- `apify-youtube` - offer if research or content showed up in their week: "Paste any YouTube link and I'll have read the whole video before you finish your coffee."
- `whatsapp-mcp` - offer if the business runs on WhatsApp: "I can search your chats - find what a client said last week without scrolling." Flag it honestly as the most involved setup; often better finished with the install team.
- Install-team tier to name (one sentence each, never walk through): extra seats (built-in company workspace sharing) if anyone else will get a seat; `company-brain` + `worker` if they want live dashboards or work happening between conversations.

## First tasks + pack (Phase 5)

**Pack recommendation:** none by default - the owner's pack follows where their week actually goes. Use the Phase 2 task list: mostly chasing follow-through and meetings → offer `ops` ("Meeting notes become owner-assigned actions, processes become documents, and nothing lapses unnoticed."); mostly hunting revenue → offer `sales-crm` ("A dossier before every call and cold outreach that reads human."); mostly making content → offer `content-marketing` ("The full content department: research, packaging, repurposing, all pre-built."); money mess → offer `finance` ("Penny-accurate statement audits and clear sign-off thresholds."). One pack to start; the rest install any day.

**First-task prompt sheet** (if they're stuck picking):
- The weekly review: "what moved, what's stuck, what's next" across clients and tasks, one screen.
- Client status update drafts in their voice.
- Meeting notes → actions on the task board.
- The pre-call brief: who am I meeting, what do we know, what's the ask.
- The inbox triage summary: what needs them vs what can wait.
