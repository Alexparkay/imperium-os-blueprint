# Start Here

Five minutes, then you never need a manual again.

## What you just installed

You have an AI operating system for your company. It looks like a chat window. Underneath it is a set of folders that hold everything the system knows about your business, plus a library of pre-built workflows called skills.

The whole mental model is three words: **chat, memory, skills.** You talk in the chat. What you say gets remembered in the right place. Skills do the repeat work. There is nothing else to learn.

## The folders you'll see

| Folder | In one line |
|---|---|
| `context/` | The one-page truth about your company. The system reads it every session. |
| `memory/` | Everything it learns: people, decisions, tasks, dates, ideas. |
| `clients/` | One folder per client engagement. |
| `content-pipeline/` | Drafts, published work, research, and your voice profile. |
| `automations/` | The running tools, like YouTube research and phone notifications. |
| `dashboard/` | Where your live company dashboard will be built. |
| `docs/` | Guides, the architecture explainer, and your setup progress page. |

You never need to open any of them. You can if you're curious. They're all plain text.

## How to talk to it

Like a sharp colleague. Full sentences, half sentences, voice-dictated rambles with three topics in one message: all fine. It sorts the pieces out. Useful habits:

- **"Remember this:"** files something permanently, in the right place.
- **"Don't do X again"** changes its behaviour for good.
- **"That doesn't sound like me"** sharpens how it writes in your voice.
- **"What do you know about [person/client/topic]?"** searches everything it has.

You do not need to write good prompts. That's its job, not yours.

## The magic sentence

Open this folder in Claude Code and type:

```
I've just installed this, let's start
```

That starts a guided setup. If you ever get interrupted, come back and type **"continue onboarding"** or **"where were we"** and it resumes exactly where you stopped.

## What the first hour looks like

1. A two-minute tour (you just read most of it).
2. It asks your name, your role, who actually sits in this chat most days, and what to keep private.
3. A short interview about the company and your corner of it: what you sell, your numbers, what you sign off yourself.
4. You paste in 2 or 3 things you've written, so everything it drafts sounds like you.
5. You plug in the tools you already use, one at a time. It tests each one.
6. It offers your department's pre-built skill pack, then you pick 3 to 5 weekly tasks and watch it do them for real.
7. It writes your week-one plan and hands you the keys.

One question at a time, plain English throughout, and a progress page (`docs/setup-status.html`, opens in your browser) that fills in as you go.

## FAQ

**Is my data private?**
Yes. Everything lives in this folder on your machine, as plain text files you can read. Nothing is shared with anyone unless you connect a service yourself during setup, and you'll be asked before each one. Your keys are stored in a local settings file that never gets backed up to the internet.

**Can I break it?**
Not by talking to it. Ask anything, paste anything, correct it bluntly. The only real rule: don't move or rename the folders by hand. If you ever want to reorganize something, ask in the chat and it will do it safely.

**Do I need to know how to code?**
No. The system runs every technical step itself. Your jobs are answering questions, clicking sign-in buttons in your browser, and telling it when something feels off.

**What if it gets something wrong?**
Tell it, the way you'd tell a person. "That's wrong, the meeting is Thursday." It fixes the mistake and remembers the correction. Every correction makes it permanently better.

**What if I get stuck?**
Just ask in the chat. It can see everything in this folder, so "why didn't the notification arrive?" or "where did that draft go?" are questions it can actually answer and fix. If it's truly stuck, contact the team who installed it for you.

**What if I close the laptop halfway through setup?**
Nothing is lost. Type "continue onboarding" next time and it picks up where you stopped.
